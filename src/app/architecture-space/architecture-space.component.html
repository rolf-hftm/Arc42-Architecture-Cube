About: Arc42 Architecture Space (3D)

As a participant in this arc42 “Software Architecture” course, you will meet many architectural terms (patterns, styles, approaches). This 3D cube is a discussion tool that helps you place those terms in a shared mental model — so the concepts become easier to compare, remember, and explain.

Important: the cube does not claim to be “scientifically correct”. The numbers are a teaching approximation to trigger meaningful conversations about trade-offs. Click a point to see a short explanation and a link for deeper reading on the right-hand side.

We map three dimensions to the X/Y/Z axes and encode the fourth dimension as point size (default: Coupling).

  • Structure: layered / hierarchical → inside-out (ports & adapters)
  • Granularity: monolith → many independently deployable units
  • Focus: technology-driven → domain-driven
  • Coupling: low coupling → high coupling (bigger point = higher value)

How to use this cube during the course

  1. Pick axes that match the topic you are currently discussing (e.g., “Structure vs. Granularity vs. Focus”).
  2. Use filters to reduce noise (categories, individual approaches, or dimensions).
  3. Click two or three approaches and compare them: “Why are they different?” “What do we gain or lose?”
  4. Relate what you see back to arc42 thinking: context, building blocks, runtime, deployment, crosscutting concerns.

Questions you can ask yourself

  • Where would our current system roughly be located in this cube — and why?
  • Which axis is our biggest pain today (structure, granularity, focus, coupling) — and what causes it?
  • What are our main quality drivers (e.g., maintainability, scalability, time-to-market, reliability)?
  • Which trade-off are we consciously accepting — and which one is accidental?
  • If we move toward “more distributed” (granularity), what new coupling appears (operations, contracts, data)?
  • What is a realistic next step for our context (e.g., Modular Monolith before Microservices)?
Tip: if you want to tweak the positions or add more terms, edit the dataset in architecture-space.component.ts. See README.md in the project root for details.
